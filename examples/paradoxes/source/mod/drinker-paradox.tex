\symdecl*{Drinker Paradox}
\begin{sparagraph}[style=symdoc]
  The \definame{Drinker Paradox} is a principle of classical predicate logic popularised by \sr{Smullyan}{Raymond Smullyan} 1978 which can be stated as:
  “There is someone in the pub such that, if he is drinking then everyone in the pub is drinking.”
\end{sparagraph}
